Provides for creating and modifying security groups. A Group is a mechanism by which Persons may be aggregated and security permissions defined for them.
A group has zero or more members. Those members are groups themselves. A group is owned by a single space. However, a group may be available in other spaces, effectively allowing its members to have privileges within that other space.
A special kind of group, called a Principal Group has Persons as its members. A Principal Group has a single owning Person and a Person may own at most one Principal Group in a space. A Principal group may have additional members. A Principal Group's name is effectively that of its owner. This allows security to be defined on groups alone and to relate a single Person to a Group within a space. It also allows additional Persons to share the permissions of the owner, by including them in the Owner's Principal Group.
Principal Groups, along with Space Administrator and Team Member groups are System Groups. A System Group's name may not be modified. Additionally, System Groups may not be removed from a space.
UserDefined Groups are groups created by a user in the application. @since gecko
